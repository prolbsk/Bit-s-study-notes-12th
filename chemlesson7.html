7.1 Introduction

  • p-block: Last electron enters p-orbital; includes Groups 13‚Äì18.
  • This chapter focuses on Groups 16 (Oxygen fam.), 17 (Halogens), 18 (Noble Gases).

7.2 Occurrence

  • Group 16: O, S, Se, Te, Po. O is most abundant (20.95% air by vol, 46.6% crust by mass). S mainly as sulfates (gypsum, baryte) & sulfides (galena, ZnS, CuFeS2).
  • Se, Te: In selenides/tellurides in ores. Po is rare & radioactive.
  • Group 17: F, Cl, Br, I, At. Never free, always as salts (e.g., F as CaF2, Na3AlF6). Sea water contains Cl, Br, I (mainly NaCl 2.5% mass). Astatine: radioactive, short-lived.
  • Group 18: He, Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe, Rn. Mostly in air (argon most), He from natural gas. Rn: decay product; rare.

7.5 Anomalous Behaviour

  • Oxygen: Diatomic (O‚ÇÇ), paramagnetic, can't show >+2 state (no d-orbitals), H‚ÇÇO is a liquid (via H-bonding), covalency up to 2 usually. Due to small size, high EN, no d.
  • Fluorine: Small size, high EN, no d-orbitals, low FF bond energy (lone pairs), always -1 O.S., forms HOF (only one oxoacid), HF liquid (H-bond), most reactions exothermic.
