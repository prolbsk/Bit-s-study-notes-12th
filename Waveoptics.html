1. Sources of Light

  • üí° Primary Sources: Produce their own light. Examples: Sun, stars, candle, bulb. Keyword: Self-luminous
  • üåï Secondary Sources: Reflect light from primary sources. Examples: Moon, mirror, water surface. Keyword: Non-luminous

2. Wavefront and Rays

  • Wavefront: Surface joining points vibrating in same phase.
  • Rays: Perpendicular to wavefront showing direction of propagation.
  • Far from source: Wavefront becomes plane (flat), rays nearly parallel.
  • Keywords: Phase, Perpendicular, Propagation, Plane wave

3. Huygens‚Äô Principle

  • Every point on a wavefront acts as a source of secondary wavelets spreading in all directions at same speed.
  • New wavefront = tangent to all secondary wavelets.
  • Keywords: Secondary wavelets, Tangent, Propagation
  • Example: Water ripple analogy üåä

4. Laws of Reflection

  • Angle of incidence = Angle of reflection (i = r)
  • Incident ray, reflected ray & normal lie in the same plane.
  • Keywords: i=r, Coplanar, Normal

5. Conditions for Steady Interference

  • Sources must be coherent (constant phase difference)
  • Light must be monochromatic (single wavelength)
  • Path difference < coherence length
  • Setup must be stable (stationary sources & screen)
  • Waves ideally same polarization
  • Keywords: Coherence, Monochromatic, Stability, Path difference

6. Optical Path Length (OPL)

Definition: Distance light effectively travels in a medium considering refractive index.

Formula: OPL = n √ó L (n = refractive index, L = actual distance)

Keywords: Refractive index, Medium, Effective distance

7. Diffraction of Light

Bending or spreading of light waves around edges of an obstacle or through a narrow slit.

Occurs when obstacle/slit size ~ wavelength of light.

Keywords: Bending, Spreading, Obstacle, Slit
